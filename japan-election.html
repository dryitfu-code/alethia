Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) is positioned for a landslide victory in the February 2026 lower house election.

Polls project the LDP will surpass the 233-seat majority threshold out of 465 total seats, rising from the current 198 seats.

The opposition Centrist Reform Alliance appears positioned to see its representation halved from the current 167 seats.

An electoral mandate would strengthen Takaichi's authority within the LDP and provide authorization to pursue her stated fiscal agenda.
