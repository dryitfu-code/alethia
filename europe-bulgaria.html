Bulgaria officially adopted the euro currency on January 1, 2026, becoming the 21st member state of the eurozone and completing its transition to Europe's common monetary system.

The adoption culminates Bulgaria's decade-long effort to meet convergence criteria following its 2007 EU accession. The enlargement strengthens the euro's position as a global currency.

Economic analysts note that euro adoption provides Bulgaria with enhanced monetary stability and closer integration with European financial markets.

The successful adoption demonstrates continued appeal of eurozone membership despite challenges.
