President Trump announced on February 2, 2026, that the United States has reached a trade agreement with India, reducing U.S. tariffs on Indian exports to 18% in exchange for India ceasing purchases of Russian crude oil. The agreement marks a significant shift in U.S.-India trade relations and reflects the administration's broader strategy to isolate Russian energy exports and deepen trade partnerships with strategic allies.

Indian officials have signaled openness to the arrangement, though details on the agreement's full scope remain under negotiation. The tariff reduction represents a more favorable rate than preliminary discussions suggested, indicating aggressive Trump administration negotiation tactics focused on geopolitical alignment alongside commercial interests.

The deal represents a major diplomatic achievement for both nations, strengthening the U.S.-India strategic partnership while providing India with improved access to American markets. Trade experts note that the agreement could reshape global energy markets by reducing demand for Russian oil.

Critics have raised questions about the agreement's impact on global oil prices and whether India will be able to quickly replace Russian crude with alternative suppliers. The administration has emphasized that the deal advances both economic and security interests by reducing Russian energy revenues.
