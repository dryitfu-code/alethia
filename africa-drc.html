Rwanda-Backed M23 Consolidates DRC Control

Rwanda-backed March 23 Movement (M23) rebels consolidated control over Uvira in South Kivu province on December 10, 2025, seizing major economic infrastructure and territorial corridors while maintaining plains control despite U.S.-brokered peace agreements.

The rebels continue territorial expansion across South Kivu, with active contestation in Mwenga and Fizi territories, contradicting pledges to withdraw following the December 4 peace accord signed by Congo President Felix Tshisekedi and Rwandan President Paul Kagame.

Plainclothes militants continue occupying Uvira city despite claimed withdrawals, engaging in clashes with local armed groups. The M23 and allied forces have seized vast territorial swathes since January 2025.

The conflict has contributed to a 70% increase in political violence across North and South Kivu in 2025 compared to 2024. Humanitarian access remains severely restricted.
