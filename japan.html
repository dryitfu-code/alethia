Prime Minister Takaichi's Party Poised for Landslide Electoral Victory

Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) is positioned for a landslide victory in the February 2026 lower house election scheduled for early February, according to polling by the Asahi newspaper. Polls project the LDP will surpass the 233-seat majority threshold out of 465 total seats, rising from the current 198 seats. Combined with coalition partner Japan Innovation Party, the ruling coalition is anticipated to secure approximately 300 seats, granting Takaichi a substantial policy mandate. The opposition Centrist Reform Alliance, the largest opposition faction, appears positioned to see its representation halved from the current 167 seats. Takaichi has emphasized expansionary fiscal policies despite concerns from some economists regarding Japan's existing sovereign debt burden.
