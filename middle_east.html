Gaza Ceasefire Progresses; Palestinians Return for First Time in Two Years

Palestinians began returning to Gaza for the first time in nearly two years on February 3, 2026, following the implementation of the U.S.-brokered ceasefire agreement between Israel and Hamas that commenced on October 8, 2025. The returning families reunited with relatives while simultaneously raising symbolic affirmations against permanent displacement schemes. The Rafah border crossing reopened on a limited basis on February 1, permitting evacuation of critically ill and injured persons. The ceasefire has substantially reduced lethal hostilities, though Israel maintains operational freedom across Gazan territory and continues security operations at reduced intensity. The second phase of the comprehensive peace plan, addressing Hamas weapons decommissioning and establishment of an international stabilization force, remains under negotiation.

Iran-EU Tensions Escalate; IRGC Listed as Terrorist Entity

The European Union formally designated Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps as a terrorist organization on January 30, 2026, triggering rapid Iranian retaliation. Iranian lawmakers gathered in parliament on February 2 wearing IRGC uniforms and chanting anti-Western slogans. The Iranian government reciprocally declared European armies "terrorist groups" in response to the designation. The escalation follows violent government crackdowns on public protests inside Iran, during which security forces killed numerous demonstrators.

Israeli Security Forces Kill Multiple Palestinians; Systematic Demolitions Continue

The scale and scope of demolitions have prompted international scrutiny regarding civilian protection standards and compliance with international humanitarian law.
