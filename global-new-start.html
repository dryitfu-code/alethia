New START Treaty Expires; Nuclear Stability in Question

The New START treaty, the last remaining strategic nuclear arms limitation agreement between the United States and Russia, expired on February 3, 2026, removing legal constraints on deployed strategic nuclear warheads and delivery systems.

The treaty, signed in 2010, limited both nations to 1,550 deployed warheads and required mutual inspections. Neither country has announced an alternative framework to replace the accord.

U.S. and Russian military officials have conducted minimal talks regarding a successor agreement, raising concerns among arms control analysts about escalated nuclear risk during the ongoing conflict in Ukraine.

International observers have called for emergency negotiations to prevent an unfettered arms race.
