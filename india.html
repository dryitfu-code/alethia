Trump-Modi Trade Deal Reduces U.S. Tariffs; India to End Russian Oil Purchases

President Trump and Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced a bilateral trade agreement on February 2, 2026, establishing U.S. tariffs on Indian exports at 18% in exchange for India's commitment to cease crude oil purchases from Russia. The accord represents a significant shift in U.S.-India trade relations and reflects the Trump administration's strategy to isolate Russian energy revenues while strengthening partnerships with strategic Asian allies. Indian officials characterized the agreement as favorable to long-term bilateral cooperation, though full details of tariff schedules across commodity sectors remain under negotiation. The accord signals India's willingness to balance economic interests with geopolitical alignment alongside the United States, while managing relationships with Russia and other regional partners.

Union Budget 2026 Emphasizes Semiconductor Manufacturing and Skill Development

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman presented India's Union Budget 2026 on February 1, with emphasis on semiconductor manufacturing capacity, doubled electronics components scheme funding, and a fourfold increase in skill development ministry allocations. The budget reinforces India's "Make in India" initiative and targets infrastructure development, particularly underground rail connectivity to northeastern regions. The budget allocates substantial resources to the Semiconductor Mission 2.0, reflecting government prioritization of domestic electronics manufacturing and technology sovereignty.

The cricket boycott reflects broader deterioration in India-Pakistan diplomatic relations and demonstrates how regional political tensions extend into sporting domains.

A Nipah virus outbreak was confirmed in West Bengal on January 21, 2026, with at least two initial cases recorded in Barasat. State health authorities and the Indian Council of Medical Research mobilized epidemiological investigation teams to trace contacts and contain spread. Nipah, a zoonotic virus transmitted from fruit bats to humans, carries significant mortality risk and limited therapeutic options.
