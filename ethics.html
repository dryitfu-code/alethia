Editorial Principles

Our editorial standards are designed to promote transparency, accuracy, and intellectual honesty in news coverage.

Core Principles

1. Accuracy Over Speed

We prioritize getting information right over being first. Breaking news is marked as "developing" until key facts can be verified across multiple sources.

2. Source Transparency

We disclose which news outlets we reference for each story and indicate their general editorial positioning. This allows readers to assess perspective diversity for themselves.

3. Clear Separation of Facts and Analysis

We distinguish between factual reporting and interpretive analysis. When presenting multiple perspectives, we do so without favoring one over another.

4. Neutral Language

We avoid loaded terminology, inflammatory language, and editorializing in news summaries. Strong language appears only in direct quotes clearly attributed to sources.

5. Context and Background

Major events don't occur in isolation. We provide relevant context and background to help readers understand why events matter and how they connect to broader patterns.

6. No Hidden Agendas

Alethia News does not promote political, ideological, or commercial interests. We have no advertisers, sponsors, or parent organizations that might influence coverage.

What We Acknowledge

Perfect objectivity is impossible. Story selection, word choice, and source selection all involve judgment calls. We address this by:

  • Making our methodology visible and documented
  • Consulting sources across the political spectrum
  • Being transparent about our limitations
  • Welcoming feedback and corrections

Corrections Policy

When we make errors, we correct them promptly and transparently. Corrections are noted at the top of affected articles with an explanation of what was changed and why. We do not silently edit or remove content.
